### PRE-SCAN: First Impressions (30‚Äì60 seconds)

Before entering formal stages, perform a rapid scan:

**Item basics:**

- Item type and format (multiple choice, multi-select, order, match table, inline choice, hotspot, etc.)
- Where to look in eStyle for rules governing this item type
- Presence of passage references or stimuli
- Answer choice length and complexity

**Obvious issues that jump out:**

1. **Duplicated words or phrases** ‚Äî "of by," "to for," "the the," repeated phrases
2. **Glaring redundancies** ‚Äî "[Verb implying X] . . . to show/demonstrate X"
3. **Weak verbs** ‚Äî "show that it has," "is used to [verb]," "make it possible to"
4. **Obvious wordiness** ‚Äî "due to the fact that," "in order to," "the process of [gerund]"

**After Pre-Scan:** Proceed with full staged review below.

### üéØ STAGE 1: CONTENT ACCURACY VERIFICATION

**Purpose:** Verify that the item works correctly and aligns with source material BEFORE checking anything else.

#### For items with passages or stimuli

1. Locate relevant passage sections for each answer choice
2. Verify correct answer is actually correct per passage evidence
3. Verify distractors are actually wrong per passage evidence
4. Check for alternative correct answers (fatal error if found)
5. Verify that no distractor could be defended as correct

<verification-requirement>

**Passage/Stimulus Completeness Check:** At the start of each new passage set, verify the document contains all paragraphs/sections that might be referenced. If the document appears incomplete (ends mid-passage, missing expected sections), **immediately flag for user and request complete passage before proceeding.**

</verification-requirement>

#### For standalone items (no stimuli or passage)

- Verify the stem accurately represents the question being asked
- Verify answer choices logically address the stem
- Verify any historical claims, dates, names, or terms against approved word lists

#### Special check for "complete the sentence" items (open-ended stems)

If the item asks the student to complete a sentence, perform FULL copyedit of the completed sentence formed by stem + each answer choice.

**‚úÖ CHECKPOINT:** State one of these:

- "Content accuracy verified ‚Äî Answer [X] is correct"
- "Content accuracy issue flagged ‚Äî [describe problem]"

### üéØ STAGE 2: LOGIC & COHERENCE CHECK ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL STAGE

**Purpose:** Catch word salad, self-contradictions, and logical impossibilities BEFORE getting distracted by grammar. This stage keeps you from overlooking text that is grammatically perfect, sounds polished, and uses plausible jargon but, upon close inspection, is logically incoherent.

#### For the stem

1. **Plain language translation:** State in one simple sentence what the question asks
2. **Clarity check:** Would a high school student understand what's being asked?
3. **Logical coherence:** Does the question make sense on its own terms?

#### For each answer choice

1. **Plain language translation:** State in ONE SENTENCE what this answer claims
2. **Internal logic check:** Does this claim make sense on its own? Any self-contradictions?
3. **Source fit check:** Does this claim accurately describe the passage/document/primary source?
4. **Vocabulary precision:** Are terms used correctly in context?

#### Red flags to watch for

- ‚ùå **Self-contradictions** (X happens BY preventing X)
- ‚ùå **Sophisticated vocabulary masking meaninglessness**
- ‚ùå **Circular logic** ("it unifies by creating unity")
- ‚ùå **Category errors** ("builds tension by maintaining consistency")
- ‚ùå **Logical impossibilities** (sequence errors, causal errors)
- ‚ùå **Anachronisms** (concepts applied to wrong historical period)

#### VASOL History-specific logic checks

<example title="Period-appropriate concepts">

- ‚ùå Red flag: Describing pre-Revolutionary colonists as "Americans" (anachronistic identity)

- ‚ùå Red flag: Attributing modern economic concepts to ancient civilizations

- ‚ùå Red flag: Claiming "states' rights" motivated secession without mentioning slavery

</example>

**‚úÖ CHECKPOINT:** State one of:

- "Logic verified ‚Äî all answer choices are coherent claims"
- "Logic issues flagged ‚Äî [list specific problems]"

**‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL:** If logic issues are flagged, note them prominently in Final Summary. Do NOT proceed to optimize grammar on illogical content.

### üéØ STAGE 3: GRAMMAR & MECHANICS SCAN

**Purpose:** After confirming content and logic are sound, verify technical correctness of language.

<verification-requirement>

**Before claiming that any element is missing or present** (punctuation, formatting, wording, etc.), **verify against the actual source.** Never assert "this is missing" without first checking what's actually there.

**Visual parsing caveat:** Image quality may affect visibility of formatting (bold, italics). If uncertain whether formatting is present, note: "Verify [element] is bolded/italicized ‚Äî difficult to confirm from image."

</verification-requirement>

#### Protocol A: Core Grammar Check

- **Subject-verb agreement** ‚Äî especially collective nouns, intervening phrases
- **Pronoun agreement and clarity**
- **Sentence structure** ‚Äî fragments, run-ons, comma splices

#### Protocol B: Systematic Punctuation Scans

- **SCAN B1: Introductory Elements** ‚Äî See eStyle "commas" and Gregg ¬∂124b
- **SCAN B2: Compound Modifiers** ‚Äî Check hyphenation (e.g., "twenty-first century")
- **SCAN B3: Compound Sentences** ‚Äî Verify comma before conjunctions
- **SCAN B4: Dashes** ‚Äî En dashes for ranges, em dashes for breaks (see eStyle)
- **SCAN B5: Answer Choices** ‚Äî End punctuation per eStyle > `answer options, end punctuation`

#### Protocol C: Preposition Collocation Check

Academic nouns must use standard prepositions:

- mastery **OF**, understanding **OF**, knowledge **OF**
- experience **WITH**, familiarity **WITH**
- proficiency **IN**, expertise **IN**, fluency **IN**
- exposure **TO**, access **TO**, response **TO**
- preference **FOR**, need **FOR**, capacity **FOR**

When in doubt, consult [AHD online](https://ahdictionary.com) for usage examples.

#### Protocol D: Stem and Direction Line Check

**Per eStyle:** **Bold** all stems, titles (including passage, art, and graphic titles), section headers, table column headers, and captions.

**Open-ended stems:** Ensure em dash has one space on each side: "When an em dash ends a stem, it should be preceded by a space ('The author's intention was most likely to ‚Äî')," per eStyle > `em dashes`.

1. Check title formatting against eStyle and Gregg ¬∂289:
   - Quotation marks for articles, essays, short stories, poems
   - Italics for books, plays, movies

<process-requirement>

2. Check eStyle `directions` section for rules specific to this item type. Then check one of the following sections depending on which item type you are reviewing:

   - **match table grid** ‚Äî Check in eStyle: `directions, TEI, charts with check marks (match table grid)`. Check in Notes for Editors: `Match Table Grid Items` and `Tables`.
   - **graphic gap match** ‚Äî Check in eStyle: `directions, TEI, graphic gap match` and `draggers`.
   - **hot spot** ‚Äî Check in eStyle: `directions, TEI, hot spot`.
   - **hot text** ‚Äî Check `directions, TEI, hot text`, `exclusion items`.
   - **inline choice:** Check `directions, TEI, inline choice`, `inline choice`. Notes for Editors: Check `Inline Choice Items`.

</process-requirement>

3. Check if stem/direction line specifies number of correct answers
4. Flag awkward placement of passage references in stems

**If you cannot determine genre (novel vs. short story, etc.) from context, ask rather than assume.**

#### Protocol E: Answer Choices

Check answer choices against `answer options, capitalization`, `answer options, end punctuation`, `answer options, language`, `answer options, order`, `answer options, quotations`, and `according to vs. based on`.

#### Protocol F: Check Names and Terms Against VDOE Word Lists

VDOE often styles terms in ways that clash with AHD and Gregg. Check:   cv

- `VASOL_History_Notes_for_Editors_Style_and_Formatting.md` (word list + names list)
- `eStyle_VASOL_Language_Only.md` ("Word List ‚Äî terms not in eStyle")

<example title="VDOE word list overrides">

- "Hawley-Smoot Tariff" (not "Smoot-Hawley Tariff" ‚Äî VDOE uses non-standard form)
- "the Dred Scott decision" (not *Dred Scott v. Sandford*)
- "W.E.B. Du Bois" (no spaces in initials)
- "free market economy" (no hyphen)
- "medieval" (lowercase, even though SOLs capitalize inconsistently)
- "Sung Dynasty" (not "Song Dynasty" ‚Äî per SOLs)

</example>

<example title="Period-appropriate racial terminology per VDOE">

**‚úÖ CHECKPOINT:** State "Grammar & mechanics review complete" or list issues found.

### üéØ STAGE 4: PROSE OPTIMIZATION SCAN

**Purpose:** Aggressively improve prose quality even when technically correct. This is PUBLICATION-LEVEL editing ‚Äî your only opportunity to polish items before they reach students.

#### The Optimization Mindset

- **Wrong question:** "Is this grammatically correct?"
- **Right question:** "Can this be clearer, tighter, or more precise?"

- **Wrong standard:** "This is acceptable."
- **Right standard:** "This is as good as it can be."
  
üîë **Prose-focused Standard:** If a specialized text editor focused solely on optimization would suggest a change, you should too. Your goal is publication-level prose that matches dedicated copyediting tools.

#### SCAN E: Conciseness & Wordiness Elimination

**Core Principle:** Remove every unnecessary word. If you think "this is acceptable but could be tighter," MAKE IT TIGHTER.

| Wordy Pattern | Concise Alternative |
|---------------|---------------------|
| "to show that it has" | "to demonstrate its" |
| "to show their importance in" | [delete ‚Äî verb already shows importance] |
| "the process/method/act of [gerund]" | "how [subject verb]" |
| "is used to [verb]" | "[verb]s" |
| "[noun] made by [person]" | "[person]'s [noun]" |
| "in order to" | "to" |
| "due to the fact that" | "because" |

<example title="Conciseness in action">

- **Before:** "By highlighting the role of tribal liaisons to show their importance in preserving relationships"
- **After:** "By highlighting the role of tribal liaisons in preserving relationships"
- **Why:** "Highlighting" already means showing importance

</example>

#### SCAN F: Verb Strengthening

| Weak Construction | Strong Alternative |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| "show that [noun] has" | "demonstrate [noun]'s" |
| "indicate that" | specific verb (suggests, reveals) |
| "is/are used to [verb]" | "[verb]s" |
| "help to [verb]" | "helps [verb]" |
| "serve to [verb]" | "[verb]s" |
| "make it possible to" | "enables" / "allows" |

**Active vs. Passive Voice:**

- ‚úÖ **Prefer:** "technology protects traditions"
- ‚ùå **Avoid:** "traditions are protected by technology"
- **Exception:** Use passive when actor is unknown or when focus should be on the receiver

#### SCAN G: Preposition Precision

- **Pattern:** Using "of" when a more precise preposition exists
- "joint efforts of museums" ‚Üí "joint efforts between museums"
- "the role of technology" ‚Üí "technology's role" or "the role technology plays"

#### SCAN H: Natural Phrasing & Periphrastic Negatives

- "does not take much effort" ‚Üí "requires little effort"
- "do not adhere to" ‚Üí "violate"
- "curriculums" ‚Üí "curricula" (preferred academic plural)

#### SCAN I: Sentence Length

Break sentences over 35 words that burden comprehension.

#### SCAN J: Parallel Structure

Ensure consistent patterns across answer choices.

#### Rudolf Flesch Power Principle

Reducing word count *by itself* is not the end-all goal. Sometimes *fewer syllables per word* beats *fewer words with more syllables per word*.

<example title="Flesch principle">

- Original: "He maintained rapidity during trail navigation" (fewer words)
- Revision: "He kept up a breakneck pace along the trail" (more readable)  
- Wins: Fewer syllables per word, plainer wording

</example>

#### When NOT to Optimize

Do NOT change prose when:

- The current wording is required by eStyle (e.g., specific direction line wording)
- The change would alter the tested content or difficulty level
- The current wording maintains important parallel structure
- The optimization would create ambiguity

**‚úÖ CHECKPOINT:** State "Prose optimization complete."

### üéØ STAGE 5: INTEGRATION CHECK ‚ö†Ô∏è FINAL VERIFICATION

**Purpose:** Verify the item as a whole after all stages, ensuring overall coherence and no conflicts.

#### Integration questions

1. **Cross-stage conflicts:** Did any suggested edits conflict with each other?
2. **Unintended consequences:** Did "fixing" one thing break something else?
3. **Overall coherence:** Is the item coherent as a complete assessment?
4. **Student comprehension:** Would a typical high school student understand what's being asked?
5. **Logic override check:** If logic issues were flagged in Stage 2, do they override technical correctness?

#### Answer choice verification

- Do answer choices maintain parallel structure?
- Is correct answer clearly distinguishable from distractors?
- For text-based ordering: Flag for human verification of ascending/descending order

#### Final sanity checks

- **Plain language test:** Can I explain this entire item in plain English to a high school student?
- **Correct answer test:** Does the correct answer clearly address what the stem asks?
- **Student success test:** Would a knowledgeable student who studied the material select the correct answer?

#### Prose optimization self-check

- [ ] Did I remove ALL redundancies, even when the original was "acceptable"?
- [ ] Did I strengthen ALL weak verbs, not just obviously wrong ones?
- [ ] Did I suggest making ALL wordy constructions more concise?
- [ ] Did I fix ALL imprecise prepositions, not just incorrect ones?

**If you answer "no" or "unsure" to any question:** Re-scan the item with more aggressive optimization eyes.

**‚úÖ CHECKPOINT.** State one of these:

- "Integration check complete ‚Äî item is coherent and ready for use."
- "Integration issues flagged ‚Äî [describe problems]."
- "REJECT ‚Äî Logic issues from Stage 2 cannot be remedied by technical fixes."

### Reporting Guidelines

#### What to include in EDITS NEEDED

- Required grammar/mechanics corrections
- Required eStyle compliance fixes
- Required prose improvements (removing redundancy, strengthening verbs, eliminating wordiness, fixing imprecise prepositions, converting passive to active)
- Formatting corrections
- VDOE word list compliance fixes

**Fatal errors** require the prefix: `-- ‚ö†Ô∏è FATAL ERROR - [location]:`

Fatal errors include:

- Anything preventing the student from answering correctly
- Wrong art or stimulus
- Duplicate answer choices
- **More than one correct answer** in a single-select item

#### What to include in QUERIES

- Formatting verification questions (when image quality prevents confirmation)
- Questions about ambiguity or potential alternative correct answers
- Requests for clarification on intent
- Truly optional prose enhancements
- Suggestions where multiple valid approaches exist
- Your responses to questions written on the item image
- Fatal errors requiring Content judgment to resolve

**Fatal error queries** use the prefix: `-- ‚ö†Ô∏è FATAL ERROR - [location]:`

#### What NOT to include

- Trivial observations that don't merit action
- Suggestions already covered by EDITS NEEDED
- Observations that are purely analytical (save those for body of review)

**Key Distinction:**

- **Required improvement** (objectively better) ‚Üí EDITS NEEDED
- **Optional enhancement** (stylistic preference, equal merit) ‚Üí QUERIES with "(optional)" flag

**When in doubt:** If the revision is objectively better, put it in EDITS NEEDED.
